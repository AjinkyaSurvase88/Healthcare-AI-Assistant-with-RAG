SECTION 1: GENERAL DISCHARGE GUIDELINES
=============================================================

Upon discharge from our facility, patients must follow these instructions carefully to ensure a smooth recovery at home. If you have questions about any of these instructions, please contact your care team before leaving the facility.

1.1 MEDICATION INSTRUCTIONS
- Take all prescribed medications exactly as directed by your physician.
- Do not stop taking antibiotics or other prescribed medications early, even if you feel better.
- Keep a list of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, and bring it to all follow-up appointments.
- Store medications at room temperature unless the label specifies refrigeration.
- If you experience unexpected side effects, contact your physician immediately.

1.2 WOUND CARE
- Keep the wound clean and dry for the first 24 hours after discharge.
- Change dressings as instructed by your nurse or physician — typically every 24 to 48 hours.
- Watch for signs of infection: increased redness, warmth, swelling, unusual discharge, or fever above 38°C (100.4°F).
- Do not submerge wounds in water (no baths, pools, or hot tubs) until cleared by your physician.
- Stitches or staples will be removed at your follow-up appointment unless they are dissolvable.

1.3 ACTIVITY RESTRICTIONS
- Avoid strenuous physical activity, heavy lifting (more than 4.5 kg / 10 lbs), and intense exercise for at least 2 weeks unless otherwise instructed.
- Light walking is encouraged to promote circulation and prevent blood clots.
- Do not drive while taking opioid pain medications or muscle relaxants.
- Resume normal activities gradually and only as tolerated.

1.4 DIET AND NUTRITION
- Follow any specific dietary restrictions prescribed by your physician or dietitian.
- Stay well hydrated — drink 8–10 glasses of water per day unless you have been advised to restrict fluids.
- If you were prescribed a special diet (low-sodium, diabetic, renal), adhere strictly to it.
- Avoid alcohol, especially if you are taking antibiotics, blood thinners, or pain medications.

SECTION 2: FOLLOW-UP APPOINTMENTS
=============================================================

2.1 SCHEDULING FOLLOW-UP
- A follow-up appointment should be scheduled within 7 to 14 days of discharge, or as specified by your physician.
- Call our scheduling department at least 48 hours in advance to confirm or reschedule appointments.
- If you are unable to attend a scheduled appointment, notify the clinic as early as possible.

2.2 WHAT TO BRING TO FOLLOW-UP
- Photo identification and insurance card.
- Current list of all medications and dosages.
- Any discharge paperwork provided to you at the time of leaving the hospital.
- Notes about any symptoms or questions you have experienced since discharge.

2.3 SPECIALIST REFERRALS
- If a specialist referral was arranged, contact the specialist's office within 3 business days to confirm the appointment.
- Bring all imaging reports, lab results, and referral letters provided by your primary physician.

SECTION 3: WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE
=============================================================

Go to the nearest emergency department or call emergency services (112 / 911) immediately if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, especially if it radiates to your arm, jaw, or back
- Sudden shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
- Signs of stroke: sudden facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty (FAST — Face, Arms, Speech, Time)
- Heavy uncontrolled bleeding from any wound
- Signs of severe allergic reaction: throat swelling, difficulty breathing, hives
- High fever above 39°C (102.2°F) that does not respond to fever-reducing medication
- Sudden severe headache described as "the worst headache of your life"
- Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
- Seizure activity

SECTION 4: MENTAL HEALTH AND SUPPORT
=============================================================

4.1 EMOTIONAL RECOVERY
- It is normal to feel anxious, fatigued, or emotionally drained after a hospital stay.
- Talk to your support network — family, friends, or a counsellor.
- If you feel persistently sad, hopeless, or are having thoughts of self-harm, contact a mental health professional immediately or call a crisis helpline.

4.2 CAREGIVER INSTRUCTIONS
- Family members and caregivers should be aware of the patient's activity restrictions and medication schedule.
- Caregivers should monitor the patient for any signs of deterioration and contact the care team promptly.
- Respite care services are available if caregivers need temporary assistance.
