SECTION 1: UNDERSTANDING INSURANCE ELIGIBILITY
=============================================================

Q1: What does "insurance eligibility" mean?
A: Insurance eligibility refers to whether you are currently enrolled in a health insurance plan and whether your plan covers a specific healthcare service or provider. Before scheduling services, our billing department verifies your eligibility with your insurance company to confirm coverage.

Q2: How do I know if I am eligible for coverage?
A: You can verify your eligibility by:
- Calling the Member Services number on the back of your insurance card
- Logging into your insurer's online portal or app
- Contacting our billing department, which will verify your eligibility before your appointment
- Requesting a Benefits Verification letter from your insurer

Q3: When is my eligibility verified?
A: Our staff verifies insurance eligibility:
- Before your first appointment with a new provider
- Before any scheduled procedure or surgery
- At the beginning of each calendar year when benefits reset
- If you report a change in your insurance coverage

SECTION 2: COVERAGE QUESTIONS
=============================================================

Q4: What services are typically covered by insurance?
A: Covered services vary by plan, but commonly include:
- Preventive care (annual physicals, vaccinations, cancer screenings) — often covered at 100%
- Primary care visits — subject to copay or deductible
- Specialist visits — usually require a copay; may require prior authorization
- Emergency room visits — covered but may have a higher cost-share
- Inpatient hospitalization — covered after deductible
- Prescription medications — covered per the plan's formulary tier
- Lab tests and imaging — usually covered; some may require prior authorization
- Mental health services — covered under parity laws in most plans
- Telehealth services — increasingly covered; check your plan for details

Q5: What is a deductible?
A: A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket for covered services before your insurance begins to pay. For example, if your deductible is ₹10,000 and you receive a service costing ₹15,000, you pay ₹10,000 and your insurance covers the rest (minus any copay or coinsurance). Preventive services are typically exempt from the deductible.

Q6: What is a copay versus coinsurance?
A: 
- Copay: A fixed amount you pay for a covered service (e.g., ₹500 for a primary care visit).
- Coinsurance: A percentage you pay after meeting your deductible (e.g., you pay 20%, insurance pays 80%).

Q7: What is an out-of-pocket maximum?
A: The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you will have to pay for covered services in a plan year. After you reach this limit, your insurance pays 100% of covered services for the rest of the year.

SECTION 3: IN-NETWORK VS OUT-OF-NETWORK
=============================================================

Q8: What does "in-network" mean?
A: In-network providers have a contract with your insurance company and have agreed to negotiated rates. Seeing an in-network provider typically results in lower out-of-pocket costs for you.

Q9: What happens if I see an out-of-network provider?
A: If you see an out-of-network provider, your insurance may cover a smaller portion of the cost, or none at all, depending on your plan type:
- HMO (Health Maintenance Organization): Generally no coverage for out-of-network care except emergencies.
- PPO (Preferred Provider Organization): Partial coverage for out-of-network; higher cost-share.
- EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization): No out-of-network coverage except emergencies.

Q10: How do I verify if a provider is in-network?
A: You can verify in-network status by:
- Using the "Find a Doctor" tool on your insurer's website
- Calling Member Services on your insurance card
- Asking our billing department to verify before your visit

SECTION 4: PRIOR AUTHORIZATION
=============================================================

Q11: What is prior authorization (PA)?
A: Prior authorization (also called pre-authorization or pre-certification) is approval from your insurance company before you receive certain services, medications, or procedures. Without PA for services that require it, your insurance may deny coverage.

Q12: Which services commonly require prior authorization?
A: Services that typically require prior authorization include:
- Non-emergency surgeries and procedures
- Advanced imaging (MRI, CT scans, PET scans)
- Specialty medications (biologics, brand-name drugs on higher tiers)
- Inpatient hospital admissions (except emergency)
- Durable medical equipment
- Home health services
- Some specialist referrals (in HMO plans)

Q13: Who is responsible for obtaining prior authorization?
A: The responsibility can vary:
- Your physician's office typically submits the request for procedures and medications.
- For referrals in an HMO, your primary care physician submits the referral.
- You are responsible for confirming that PA was obtained before the service.

SECTION 5: CLAIMS AND BILLING
=============================================================

Q14: What is an EOB (Explanation of Benefits)?
A: An EOB is a statement from your insurance company that explains how a claim was processed. It shows:
- The total amount billed
- The amount the insurance paid
- The amount you owe
- The reason for any denied charges

Q15: What should I do if my claim is denied?
A: If your claim is denied:
1. Review the denial reason on the EOB.
2. Contact your insurance company to understand the denial.
3. Request an appeal in writing within the timeframe specified (usually 60–180 days).
4. Ask your physician's office to submit a letter of medical necessity if applicable.
5. Contact our billing department for assistance — we often help patients navigate appeals.

Q16: Can I get a cost estimate before receiving care?
A: Yes. Our billing department can provide a Good Faith Estimate (GFE) for scheduled services. This estimate is based on the expected services, your insurance benefits, and historical pricing. Actual charges may vary.

SECTION 6: SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
=============================================================

Q17: What if I lose my insurance coverage?
A: If you lose coverage, you may qualify for:
- COBRA continuation coverage (for employer-sponsored plans; 18 months of continued coverage at full premium cost)
- Special Enrollment Period on the Marketplace (60 days from loss of coverage)
- Medicaid or government-sponsored programs if you meet income requirements
- Our facility's financial assistance program for uninsured or underinsured patients

Q18: Do you offer financial assistance for uninsured patients?
A: Yes. Our facility offers a charity care program and sliding-scale fee assistance based on income. Please contact our financial counseling department to apply.
